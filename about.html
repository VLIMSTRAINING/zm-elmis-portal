About eLMIS Zambia

A national, open-source Electronic Logistics Management Information System — the single source of truth for logistics data and supply chain performance across Zambia's public health system.

Background

Zambia's national health supply chain

Zambia's public health supply chain spans thousands of facilities across geographically diverse and logistically complex environments, supporting HIV, TB, malaria, laboratory, and essential medicine programs with high performance expectations and accountability requirements. eLMIS was implemented to create a unified national platform serving as a single source of truth for logistics data and supply chain performance.

Global Context

How a global collaboration gave birth to eLMIS in Zambia

2006

Rockefeller Foundation e-Health Summit sparks global momentum for shared logistics tooling.

2008

Common Requirements for LMIS published in four languages.

2010

VillageReach, JSI, PATH & the Rockefeller Foundation create OpenLMIS.

2011

2012

66 countries apply the HMN Health Information Systems assessment framework.

System Overview

Enterprise-grade, open source, user-centred

Built on OpenLMIS — transparent, community-driven, with no vendor lock-in. Highly configurable across programs, products and facility types, API-enabled for integration with the Warehouse Management System (WMS) and the national EHR, and guided throughout by Green Tree, user-centred design principles.

Facility Edition

1,710 Health Facilities

Deployed to health facilities across Zambia for day-to-day inventory and dispensing.

  • Electronically manage storeroom commodities
  • Record consumption data accurately
  • Monitor lab equipment functionality
  • Dispense medicines to clients electronically
  • Generate reports & requisitions (R&R)
Central / Web Edition

Nationwide Coverage

National-level oversight, aggregation and planning across the whole supply chain.

  • National-level supply chain oversight
  • Aggregates data from all Facility Editions
  • Integrated with WMS & national EHR via APIs
  • Supports national procurement planning
  • Role-based access for all stakeholder levels
Facility-Level Workflow

Six steps from commodity receipt to report submission

  1. Receive — receive commodities from suppliers or higher-level warehouses.
  2. Issue — issue commodities to internal nodes (dispensary, lab, VCT, ART).
  3. Dispense — dispense medicines to clients electronically with full traceability.
  4. Adjustments — record expiries, damages or corrections to maintain accuracy.
  5. Physical Count — conduct physical inventory counts across all nodes and reconcile.
  6. Report & Requisition — the system auto-generates R&Rs from captured data for district review.

The Fundamental Objective

The Six Rights of a well-functioning logistics system

Every feature of eLMIS is designed to achieve one core mission — getting the right products to the right people.

RIGHT QUANTITIES

Neither too much nor too little — correct stock levels at all times.

RIGHT COMMODITIES

The specific products patients need, matched to clinical requirements.

RIGHT CONDITION

Stored and transported correctly to maintain quality and efficacy.

RIGHT PLACE

Reaching the specific facility where the products are needed.

RIGHT TIME

Arriving before stock is depleted so service delivery is never disrupted.

RIGHT COST

An efficient supply chain that minimizes waste and delivers value.
